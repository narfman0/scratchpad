# THE DROWNING ROAD
### A One-Shot D&D Adventure for Two Players

---

## RULES (tell the players this)
- Roll the d20 when I ask. **10 or higher = success.** Under 10 = something goes sideways, but you keep going.
- Each player starts with **10 HP**. Cross off ticks when hurt.
- No wrong answers. Just tell me what your character does.

---

## THE CHARACTERS

**Player 1 (older — horses)**
Name: _______ | HP: oooooooooo
Special: Riding horse named **Stormcloud** (grey, brave, hates water)
Item: *(chosen at Dr. Coral's)*

**Player 2 (younger — sea creatures)**
Name: _______ | HP: oooooooooo
Special: Marine kit — glowing jar, notebook, hermit crab named **Noodle**
Item: *(chosen at Dr. Coral's)*

---

## THE VILLAIN
**Mister Finch** — rich collector who wants a sea serpent for his private aquarium. Not evil, just selfish. Hired a crew to trap one with a net. He doesn't care that it's destroying the tides.
**Weakness: terrified of horses.**
HP: 8 | Crew members: 5 HP each

---

## ACT 0 — SOLO OPENINGS
*Send one player out of the room for each scene.*

**Player 1 — The Stables**
Every horse is panicking — a sound under the ocean only animals can hear.
Task: Calm 3 horses before they hurt themselves.
- Creative solution = automatic success
- Roll for each one: 10+ works cleanly, under 10 = the horse bolts and she chases it
End: Stormcloud nudges her toward Dr. Coral's cottage.

**Player 2 — The Tide Pool**
Her favorite tide pool: all the fish are gone, water smells wrong, a huge net anchored offshore.
Task: Find 3 clues (just describe what she looks at).
1. Net has a maker's mark — crab-in-a-circle symbol
2. Dead jellyfish floating — something big disturbed the deep
3. A giant iridescent scale caught in the rocks
Optional: A baby electric eel stuck under a rock. Free it (takes 3 HP from zap, but it becomes a companion) or wait for it to calm first (automatic, but takes time — arrives later, opens a shortcut).
End: She follows the path to Dr. Coral's cottage.

*Bring both players back.*
**"You both arrive at the blue cottage door at the exact same moment. You've never met."**
Let them introduce their characters to each other.

---

## ACT 1 — DR. CORAL'S COTTAGE
Dr. Coral: 70 years old, silver-haired, slightly chaotic. Has a trained seal named **Captain Biscuit** who claps at everything. Bad knee — can't go herself.

**What she tells them:**
- A baby sea serpent is trapped by a net in the sea cave under the east arch of the land bridge
- Its thrashing is wrecking the tides — the bridge floods permanently in a few hours
- Mister Finch set the trap. His boat is anchored at the east cliffs.
- She found the crab-in-a-circle symbol — it's Finch's crew's mark

**THE CHOICE (they must agree):**
She has two items. They're splitting up — each can only carry one.
- 🔔 **Calming Bell** — soothes panicked sea creatures (useful at the serpent)
- 🗺️ **Tide Map** — shows exactly when each bridge section floods (useful for escape timing)

**Together moment:** They ride Stormcloud across the first section of the bridge until it forks.
- Left path → east cliffs (Finch's boat)
- Right path → sea caves under the bridge
They split here.

---

## ACT 2 — SOLO TASKS
*Run one at a time. Other player watches.*

**Player 1 — Finch's Camp (east cliffs)**
Two crew members guard the boat. Finch is at the cave.
Goal: Get past the crew and find the contract (proof Finch ordered the trap).

Options:
- Distract: Ride Stormcloud loudly on the path — they come to look, she sneaks past
- Sneak: Roll — 10+ she gets through, under 10 a guard spots her (must talk her way out)
- Bluff: "Dr. Coral says the tide is coming in, move the boat" — roll to convince them

She finds the contract AND learns Finch's weakness (notes about horses in his logbook).

**Player 2 — The Sea Cave**
Finch is inside trying to add a second net. Serpent is partly trapped — one fin netted to the cave wall.
Goal: Free the fin quietly. Then get out.

Options:
- Use calming bell now: serpent goes still (easy to work) but sound alerts Finch
- Work silently: Roll — 10+ frees the fin unnoticed, under 10 serpent thrashes and Finch turns around
- Electric eel companion (if rescued earlier): zaps the net anchor, automatic success

Either way: Finch blocks the exit when he sees her. She stalls him until her sister arrives.
*"I know who you are. Dr. Coral knows too. My sister is on her way."*

---

## ACT 3 — FINAL CONFRONTATION
Player 1 rides Stormcloud into the cave entrance just as Finch has cornered Player 2.
Finch sees the horse → freezes against the wall. Now they have leverage.

**Three ways to win (player choice — no wrong answer):**

1. **Persuade:** Show the contract. Tell him Dr. Coral knows his name. Tell him the serpent is a baby.
   Roll if their argument is weak. Automatic if it's good. He backs down and leaves.

2. **Scare:** Ride Stormcloud toward him. He panics, stumbles into the water.
   Automatic success (his weakness). He leaves humiliated. Crew fishes him out.

3. **Deal:** What does he actually want? Fame. 
   Offer to let Player 2 sketch the serpent in her notebook — he gets proof without taking it.
   Automatic success if they think of this.

**If it goes to combat:**
Finch: 8 HP. Two crew: 5 HP each. Roll to attack — 10+ deals 3 damage, under 10 deals 1.
Finch surrenders at 3 HP or less.

---

## THE ENDING
They free the serpent fully. The tides calm immediately.

The serpent surfaces and does something for each player:
- **Bows its head** toward Player 2 (she understood it)
- **Bumps its nose gently against Stormcloud** — horse doesn't flinch. First time.

Dr. Coral is waiting on shore with Captain Biscuit clapping furiously.

**Gifts from the serpent** (found washed ashore):
- A **pearl** for Player 2
- A **sea-glass horseshoe** for Stormcloud (Player 1 can keep it)

Each player describes one thing their hero does or says. Roll credits.

---

## DM NOTES

**If they get stuck:**
- *"What does your character feel right now?"*
- *"You notice something in the corner — what do you look at first?"*
- *"Stormcloud / the sea creatures seem to be reacting to something. What do you do?"*

**Pacing:** Solo openings 15 min | Dr. Coral 10 min | Bridge 5 min | Solo tasks 20 min | Finale 15 min | Ending 5 min = **~70 minutes total**

**The secret:** If energy dips, skip the roll and let them succeed. The story bends around them.
